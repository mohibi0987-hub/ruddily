Heikin Ashi
What is Heikin Ashi?
Heikin Ashi is a Japanese charting technique that uses averaged price data to filter out market noise and make trends clearer and easier to read.
How it differs from regular candles
Standard candlesticks show the raw open, high, low, and close for each period. Heikin Ashi candles use a formula that averages the previous candle's values into the current one:

HA Close = (O+H+L+C) / 4
HA Open = (prev HA Open + prev HA Close) / 2
HA High = max(H, HA Open, HA Close)
HA Low = min(L, HA Open, HA Close)
Why traders use it
Trend identification — consecutive same-color candles make uptrends and downtrends immediately obvious without needing extra indicators.

Noise reduction — small reversals and wicks are smoothed out, so you avoid being shaken out of good positions by minor volatility.

Reversal signals — a candle with a small body and long wicks on both sides signals indecision and a potential trend change.

Limitation — because prices are averaged, Heikin Ashi does not reflect actual market prices. It is best used alongside real price data and is not suitable for precise entry/exit levels.
